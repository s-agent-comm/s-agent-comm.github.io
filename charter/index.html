Goals

The Semantic Agent Communication Community Group aims to define semantic models and communication structures that enable autonomous software agents to interoperate in a verifiable, accountable, and machine-interpretable manner. Existing approaches focus on transport protocols or model-level behaviors. This group focuses instead on the semantic layer required for identity, delegation, capability discovery, intent modeling, and execution traceability. The goal is to establish a common semantic foundation for interoperable agent-to-agent communication across heterogeneous systems, identity frameworks, and governance requirements.

Scope of Work

The Community Group will define the semantic structures required for agent-to-agent communication. These structures describe how identity, delegation, capabilities, intent, and execution evidence are represented and interpreted across heterogeneous agent systems. The scope includes conceptual, logical, and machine-interpretable models that support:

  • Interaction Semantics: Core definitions for Intent, Delegation, Capability advertisement, ExecutionRecord, and other CommunicativeActs used in interoperable agent communication.
  • Identity and Verifiability Primitives: Conceptual models drawing on technologies such as Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs), Verifiable Credentials (VCs), signatures, and provenance structures to establish trust in agent actions, including bindings to hardware attestation mechanisms.
  • Narrative Model: The abstract model for append-only, context-complete semantic logs that enable traceability, accountability, and explainability of agent behavior.
  • Semantic Ledger Model: A logical, implementation-agnostic model for ordered, immutable, verifiable records. No specific blockchain or distributed system architecture is implied.
  • Conformance Definitions: Minimal normative requirements for interpreting semantic structures (e.g., CommunicativeActs, Delegations) consistently across implementations.
  • Schema.org Extension Exploration: Identifying and preparing candidate extensions to Schema.org for Agent-related concepts to support broader discoverability and Web interoperability.
  • Knowledge Representation Integration: Conceptual modeling of how stateful, version-controlled knowledge representation technologies can interface with agent communication semantics. Stateless systems such as vector databases are considered insufficient for these semantic requirements and remain outside the group’s technical definition.

The CG may maintain multiple GitHub repositories under a shared organization. All repositories operate under this Charter, the CLA, and the same contribution rules.

Out of Scope

To ensure clarity of scope, the following topics are explicitly out of scope:

  • Transport Protocols: No definition or extension of HTTP, WebSockets, TCP, UDP, or QUIC.
  • Internal Agent Reasoning: No specification of LLMs, planners, inference engines, or cognitive architectures.
  • Execution Backends: No requirements for CPU, GPU, WASM, or other hardware execution models.
  • Distributed Ledger Technology: No consensus protocols or blockchain definitions.
  • Domain-Specific Ontologies: No legal, medical, or vertical knowledge models.
  • Runtime Implementations: The CG does not define SDKs, operational agents, or execution runtimes.
  • Regulatory Interpretation: The CG does not define legal norms or policy directives.

Deliverables

Specifications

The Community Group intends to publish non-normative reports, technical notes, and machine-readable artifacts including:

  • AI Agent Semantic Communication Ontology v1.0: A modular ontology published in Turtle and JSON-LD, hosted in the agent-ontology repository.
  • JSON-LD Contexts and RDF/OWL Vocabularies: Vocabulary files defining agent identity, capabilities, delegation, intent, and execution semantics.
  • SHACL Shapes for Ontology Validation: Shapes that support conformance and data quality checks.
  • AgentIDL Specification: A non-normative description of an interface description language derived from the ontology to facilitate code generation. A reference implementation MAY be produced.
  • Interoperability and Governance Notes: Guidelines for implementers, operators, and system designers adopting the semantic models.
  • Best Practices: Documentation on integrating the semantic layer with agent runtimes, identity frameworks, provenance systems, and OS-level mechanisms.

Non-Normative Reports

The group may produce additional Community Group Reports such as use cases, requirements, white papers, interoperability guides, and mapping documents.

Test Suites and Other Software

The group MAY produce test suites and example implementations to support its reports. See the GitHub LICENSE file for contribution licensing information.

Dependencies or Liaisons

The group expects to coordinate with the following:

  • Credentials Community Group
  • Decentralized Identifier Working Group
  • Verifiable Credentials Working Group
  • Web of Things Working Group
  • AI governance and safety-related community initiatives
  • External agent framework communities and implementers

Community and Business Group Process

The group operates under the Community and Business Group Process. Terms in this Charter that conflict with that Process are void.

This Community Group does not produce W3C normative standards. All reports are non-normative unless transitioned into a W3C Working Group.

W3C seeks organizational commitments under the Community Contributor License Agreement (CLA). When an individual contributes without representing an organization, W3C will seek a CLA from the relevant organization when the individual makes a contribution to a Deliverable.

The W3C Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct applies to participation in this group.

Work Limited to Charter Scope

The group will not publish Specifications outside the defined scope. See Amendments to this Charter for revision procedures.

Contribution Mechanics

Substantive contributions to Deliverables may only be made by participants who have agreed to the W3C Community Contributor License Agreement (CLA).

All outputs must use the W3C Software and Document License.

All contributions occur in the group’s GitHub repositories, via pull requests, issues, or comments on existing issues.

All repositories must include the standard CONTRIBUTING and LICENSE files.

Transparency

All technical work will occur in public GitHub repositories. Meeting minutes and summaries will be posted publicly.

Decision Process

The group will seek consensus. If consensus is unclear, a Call for Consensus (CfC) may be issued. When required for progress, Committers may select an alternative with substantial support. Objections may be addressed by maintaining alternative forks for evaluation.

Chair Selection

Participants choose their Chair(s). If five participants from distinct organizations call for an election, the group will conduct a formal election as described in the charter template, consulting the Community Development Lead as necessary.

Amendments to this Charter

The group may propose an amended charter using the Decision Process. Approval requires a 30-day vote and 2/3 support of votes cast. Minor corrections may be made through simpler group decisions.
